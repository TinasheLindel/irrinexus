Soil-water-nutrient budget
Leaching fraction · nutrient fate · groundwater contamination load · quality-adjusted recharge · Ecosystem axis input
Deep percolation from the scheduling engine carries nutrients below the root zone. The fate of those nutrients — synthetic N, organic N, wastewater-borne contaminants — determines groundwater quality and ecosystem health. This module computes the contamination load entering the aquifer under each irrigation technology × fertiliser × water quality scenario, and produces the quality-adjusted recharge volume that feeds the Ecosystem axis of the WEFEH polygon.

Water Quality Index (WQI)
Source water suitability · soil pathway transformation · receiving ecosystem quality · One Health risk — all schemes
The WQI replaces the single HSI score with a three-layer index covering irrigation suitability, ecosystem impact pathway, and human health risk. For wastewater schemes it integrates pathogen load, ARG burden, heavy metals, and pharmaceutical residues. The WQI health axis feeds directly into the WEFEH polygon as the H dimension — making water quality the explicit bridge between food production, ecosystem health, and human health under One Health logic.

WEFEH nexus polygon
Five axes: Water · Energy · Food · Ecosystem · Health — integrated One Health nexus index
The WEFEH polygon extends Taguta et al. (2022) from three axes to five. Water (WUE), Energy (EP), and Food (yield normalised) retain the original methodology. Ecosystem (quality-adjusted recharge score from Module 3) and Health (WQI from Module 4) complete the One Health nexus. The polygon area is the WEFEH index — the single integrated metric of irrigation system performance across all five dimensions simultaneously. Technology transitions are shown as polygon shifts, making the full nexus cost and benefit of modernisation visible.
